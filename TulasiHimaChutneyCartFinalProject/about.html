Frequently Asked Questions

Chutney is a condiment or sauce that originates from the Indian subcontinent, known for its vibrant flavors and versatility. It is typically made by combining various ingredients such as fruits, vegetables, herbs, spices, vinegar, sugar, and sometimes chili peppers. The mixture is then cooked down to a thick, chunky consistency or blended into a smooth paste.

Masala is a term commonly used in South Asian cuisine to refer to a blend of spices, herbs, and other seasonings that are combined to create a flavorful mixture. The word "masala" itself means "spice" in Hindi and other Indian languages. It represents a combination of various spices that work together to add depth, aroma, and taste to a wide range of dishes.

Tadka, also known as tempering or seasoning, is a cooking technique commonly used in Indian cuisine to enhance the flavor and aroma of a dish. It involves adding a mixture of spices, herbs, and sometimes other ingredients to hot oil or ghee (clarified butter) and quickly frying them. This process allows the flavors and aromas of the spices to be released and infused into the oil, which is then poured over or added to the dish, elevating its taste and adding a final touch of depth.
